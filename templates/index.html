Stepping and Footwork

The footwork rule is one of the most important concepts to master in netball. Unlike basketball, you cannot run or dribble with the ball. Once you catch the ball, you must stop and establish a landing foot.

  • Landing on one foot: If you land on one foot, that foot becomes your landing foot and must stay grounded. You can pivot on that foot, but it cannot be lifted and re-grounded. You must pass or shoot before your landing foot returns to the ground.
  • Landing on two feet: If you land on both feet simultaneously, you can choose either foot to be your landing foot. Once you move one foot, the other immediately becomes your landing foot.
  • A player can jump from the landing foot to pass or shoot, but must release the ball before landing on the ground again.

Contact and Obstruction

Netball is a non-contact sport. The rules are designed to ensure fair play and prevent injuries by strictly controlling how players can interact physically.

Contact

A player cannot make physical contact with an opponent in a way that impedes their play. This includes:

  • Pushing, tripping, holding, or leaning on another player.
  • Charging into an opponent or moving into their path to cause a collision.
  • Using the ball to push an opponent away.

Obstruction

When defending a player who has the ball, you must stand a specific distance away. This is called obstruction.

  • The minimum distance for a defender is 3 feet (0.9 metres). This is measured from the opponent's landing foot.
  • You can only attempt to intercept the ball or defend the player once you are at the correct 3-foot distance.
  • Putting your arms up to block from too close will result in an obstruction penalty.
